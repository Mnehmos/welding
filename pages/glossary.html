Welding Glossary

  • Home
  • Glossary

This comprehensive glossary provides definitions for common welding terms, acronyms, and jargon. Whether you're a beginner learning the basics or an experienced welder reviewing specifications, this reference will help clarify welding terminology.

A

AC (Alternating Current)

An electrical current that periodically reverses direction, typically used in some welding applications to control arc characteristics and reduce arc blow.

Alloy

A metal composed of two or more elements combined to provide specific properties such as strength, corrosion resistance, or heat resistance.

Amperage

The measure of electrical current flow in a welding circuit, usually expressed in amps (A). Amperage primarily controls penetration and deposition rate.

Arc

A sustained electrical discharge through a gas, producing high temperature capable of melting metal and providing the heat source for fusion welding processes.

Arc Blow

The deflection of an arc from its normal path due to magnetic forces, occurring most frequently when welding with DC near magnetized parts or at the ends of joints.

Arc Length

The distance between the tip of the electrode and the surface of the weld pool during welding.

Arc Strike

A discontinuity consisting of a small melted portion of the base metal caused by striking an arc away from the intended weld joint.

AWS

American Welding Society. A nonprofit organization that sets standards and certifies welders in various processes.

B

Back Gouging

The removal of weld material and base metal from the weld root side of a welded joint to facilitate complete fusion and penetration upon subsequent welding from that side.

Backhand Welding

A welding technique in which the welding torch or electrode holder is directed opposite to the progress of welding.

Backing

Material (metal, weld metal, carbon, etc.) placed against the back side of the joint during welding to support molten weld metal.

Base Metal

The metal or alloy that is welded, brazed, soldered, or cut.

Bead

A deposit of weld metal resulting from a single welding pass.

Bevel

An angled edge preparation on a part for a groove weld.

Burn-Through

A weld defect in which the weld metal melts through the base metal and creates a hole.

Butt Joint

A joint between two members aligned approximately in the same plane.

C

Cap Pass

The final pass of a multipass weld that establishes the weld face geometry.

Carbon Arc Cutting

An arc cutting process that uses a carbon electrode to melt the metal which is then removed by the force of the arc.

Certification

Written verification that a welder has produced welds meeting prescribed standards of performance.

Cold Lap

A weld discontinuity caused by molten weld metal flowing over unfused base metal surface without actually fusing to it.

Convexity

The maximum distance from the face of a convex fillet weld to a line joining the weld toes.

Corner Joint

A joint between two members located at approximately right angles to each other in the form of an L.

Crater

A depression at the termination of a weld bead or in the weld pool.

Current

The flow of electricity in a circuit, measured in amperes. In welding, current determines heat input and penetration.

D

DC (Direct Current)

An electrical current that flows in only one direction, commonly used in many welding processes.

DCEN (Direct Current Electrode Negative)

Direct current with the electrode connected to the negative terminal of the power source. Also called straight polarity.

DCEP (Direct Current Electrode Positive)

Direct current with the electrode connected to the positive terminal of the power source. Also called reverse polarity.

Defect

A discontinuity or discontinuities that render a part or product unable to meet minimum applicable acceptance standards or specifications.

Deposition Rate

The weight of material deposited in a unit of time, typically expressed in pounds per hour (lb/hr).

Dilution

The change in composition of a welding filler metal caused by the admixture of the base metal or previous weld metal in the weld bead. Expressed as percentage of base metal in the weld metal.

Distortion

The warping or deformation of a weldment due to the heating and cooling cycle of the welding process.

Duty Cycle

The percentage of time during a certain time period (usually 10 minutes) that a welding machine can be operated at its rated output without exceeding its temperature limits.

E

Edge Joint

A joint between the edges of two or more parallel or nearly parallel members.

Electrode

In stick welding (SMAW), a flux-coated metal rod that conducts current and provides filler metal for the weld.

Electrode Classification

A system for identifying electrodes according to their composition, mechanical properties, and usability. In AWS standards, electrodes are classified using a sequence of letters and numbers (e.g., E7018).

Electrode Holder

A device used to mechanically hold the electrode and conduct current to it during welding. Also called a "stinger".

Elongation

The increase in gauge length, expressed as a percentage of the original length, when a material is tested to rupture in tension.

F

Face of Weld

The exposed surface of a weld on the side from which welding was done.

Filler Metal

Metal added during welding, brazing, or soldering.

Fillet Weld

A weld of approximately triangular cross-section joining two surfaces approximately at right angles to each other.

Flux

A material used to prevent, dissolve, or facilitate removal of oxides and other undesirable surface substances during welding.

Forehand Welding

A welding technique in which the welding torch or electrode holder is directed toward the progress of welding. Also called "push" welding.

Fusion

The melting together of filler metal and base metal, or the melting together of base metal only, to produce a weld.

Fusion Zone

The area of base metal melted as determined on the cross section of a weld.

G

GMAW (Gas Metal Arc Welding)

A welding process that uses a continuously fed wire electrode and an externally supplied gas shield. Also known as MIG welding.

Groove Weld

A weld made in a groove between two members to be joined.

GTAW (Gas Tungsten Arc Welding)

A welding process that uses a non-consumable tungsten electrode and an externally supplied gas shield. Also known as TIG welding.

Ground Clamp

A device used to connect the work lead (ground) from the power source to the workpiece.

H

HAZ (Heat-Affected Zone)

The portion of the base metal whose properties or microstructure have been altered by the heat of welding, cutting, or thermal spraying.

Heat Input

The electrical energy supplied to the arc per unit length of weld. Typically calculated by multiplying voltage by amperage and dividing by travel speed.

Hot Pass

The second pass in a multiple-pass weld, made while the root pass is still hot, used to refine grain structure and reduce the likelihood of defects.

Hot Shortness

A tendency of some metals to separate or crack along grain boundaries when stressed or deformed at temperatures near the melting point.

Hydrogen Cracking

Cracking that occurs in the weld or HAZ due to the presence of hydrogen, residual stress, and a susceptible microstructure. Also known as cold cracking or delayed cracking.

I

Incomplete Fusion

A weld discontinuity in which fusion did not occur between weld metal and base metal or between adjoining weld beads.

Incomplete Joint Penetration

A joint root condition in a groove weld in which weld metal does not extend through the joint thickness.

Interpass Temperature

The temperature of a weld area between passes, often specified to prevent excessive heat buildup and potential metallurgical problems.

Inverter

A type of welding power source that converts AC power to DC power at a high frequency, resulting in a smaller, more efficient machine.

J

J-Groove

A groove weld with one member perpendicular to the other and having the shape of a "J".

Joint

The area where two or more members are to be joined.

Joint Clearance

The distance between the faying surfaces of a joint, usually expressed as the root opening.

Joint Design

The geometry of a joint, including the shape and dimensions of the groove and the orientation of the members.

Joint Penetration

The distance weld metal extends into the joint beyond the surface of the base metal.

K

Keyhole

A technique in which a hole is created through the joint thickness at the leading edge of the weld pool, allowing complete joint penetration in a single pass.

K-Number

An AWS designation used for grouping welding electrodes and filler materials having similar usability characteristics.

L

Lag Angle

The angle that the electrode makes with reference to a line perpendicular to the weld axis, in the direction opposite to welding progression.

Lap Joint

A joint between two overlapping members.

Lead Angle

The angle that the electrode makes with reference to a line perpendicular to the weld axis, in the direction of welding progression.

Leg of a Fillet Weld

The distance from the joint root to the toe of the fillet weld.

Low Hydrogen Electrode

An electrode with a coating formulated to introduce minimal hydrogen into the weld metal, reducing the risk of hydrogen-induced cracking.

M

Manipulation

The movement of an electrode, gun, or torch in a prescribed pattern to control bead formation and properties.

Metal Fume Fever

A flu-like illness caused by inhalation of certain metal oxides, particularly zinc oxide, during welding operations.

Multipass Weld

A weld requiring more than one pass to complete.

N

NDT (Non-Destructive Testing)

Testing methods that do not destroy the part being tested, used to detect defects in welds.

Neutral Flame

An oxyfuel gas flame adjustment with a balanced mixture of fuel gas and oxygen, used as the standard for most welding and cutting operations.

Nozzle

A device that directs shielding gas into the weld area.

O

Open Circuit Voltage (OCV)

The voltage between the output terminals of a welding power source when no current is flowing in the welding circuit.

Operating Factor

The ratio of arc time to total working time, including electrode changing, slag removal, positioning, etc.

Overhead Position

The welding position where welding is performed from the underside of a joint (4G/4F).

Overlap

A weld defect where weld metal flows over the base metal surface without fusing to it.

Oxidizing Flame

An oxyfuel gas flame adjustment with excess oxygen, producing a distinctive hissing sound and a shorter, more pointed inner cone.

P

Parent Metal

The metal to be welded, brazed, soldered, or cut. Also called base metal.

Pass

A single progression of welding along a joint. The result of a pass is a weld bead or layer.

Penetration

The distance that weld metal extends below the surface of the melted base metal. Also called joint penetration.

Porosity

Cavity-type discontinuities formed by gas entrapment during solidification of the weld metal.

Postheating

The application of heat to an assembly after welding or cutting operations.

Preheat

The heat applied to the base metal prior to welding, cutting, or thermal spraying, to achieve the specified temperature.

Pressure Vessel

A container designed to withstand internal or external pressure, requiring special welding considerations to ensure safety.

Push Angle

The electrode angle in the direction of travel, also called forehand welding technique.

Q

Quench

Rapid cooling of a metal by immersion in oil, water, or other medium.

Quench Cracking

Cracking that occurs during or after rapid cooling (quenching) from an elevated temperature.

R

Radiographic Testing (RT)

A nondestructive testing method that uses X-rays or gamma rays to detect internal discontinuities in welds.

Reinforcement

Weld metal in excess of the amount required to fill a joint.

Residual Stress

Stress present in a material that is free of external forces or thermal gradients.

Root Face

That portion of the groove face adjacent to the root of the joint.

Root Gap

The separation between the members to be joined at the root of the joint. Also called root opening.

Root Pass

The initial pass in a multipass weld that penetrates the root of the joint.

Rutile Electrode

An electrode with a coating that contains a high proportion of titanium dioxide (rutile), providing good weldability and all-position capability.

S

SAW (Submerged Arc Welding)

A welding process that uses a granular flux to cover the arc and the weld pool.

Sensitization

The process of forming chromium carbides at grain boundaries in stainless steels when heated to certain temperatures, making them susceptible to intergranular corrosion.

Shielding Gas

Protective gas used to prevent atmospheric contamination of the weld pool.

Slag

The nonmetallic byproduct of the fusion of flux with impurities in the base metal and other constituents during welding.

Slag Inclusion

A discontinuity consisting of slag entrapped in weld metal or between weld metal and base metal.

SMAW (Shielded Metal Arc Welding)

A welding process that uses a flux-covered electrode, also called stick welding.

Spatter

Metal particles expelled during welding that do not form part of the weld.

Stringer Bead

A weld bead made without appreciable weaving motion.

Stress Relief

Reduction of residual stresses in a material by heating to a suitable temperature, holding for a specific time, and then cooling slowly enough to prevent formation of new residual stresses.

T

T-Joint

A joint between two members located approximately at right angles to each other in the form of a T.

Tensile Strength

The maximum load in tension which a material can withstand before breaking, usually expressed in pounds per square inch (psi).

Throat of a Fillet Weld

The shortest distance from the weld root to the face of a fillet weld.

TIG Welding

Tungsten Inert Gas welding, another name for Gas Tungsten Arc Welding (GTAW).

Toe of Weld

The junction between the face of a weld and the base metal.

Travel Angle

The angle that the electrode makes with reference to a line perpendicular to the weld axis, in a plane determined by the electrode and the weld axis.

Travel Speed

The rate at which the arc is moved along the weld joint, usually expressed in inches per minute (ipm).

U

U-Groove

A weld joint preparation with a U-shaped cross-section.

Ultrasonic Testing (UT)

A nondestructive testing method that uses high-frequency sound waves to detect internal discontinuities in welds.

Undercut

A groove melted into the base metal adjacent to the weld toe or weld root and left unfilled by weld metal.

Underfill

A depression on the face of the weld or root surface extending below the adjacent surface of the base metal.

V

V-Groove

A groove weld with a V-shaped cross-section.

Vertical Position

The welding position where the weld axis is approximately vertical (3G/3F).

Visual Inspection

Examination of welds, materials, or components using the naked eye or simple aids like magnifiers.

Voltage

The electrical pressure or force that causes current to flow in a circuit, typically measured in volts (V). In welding, voltage primarily controls arc length and bead width.

W

Washout

The erosion or remelting of the edges of a weld pass by a subsequent pass.

Weave Bead

A weld bead made with transverse oscillation (side-to-side movement) of the electrode.

Weldability

The capacity of a material to be welded under fabrication conditions into a specific, suitably designed structure and to perform satisfactorily in the intended service.

Weld Bead

A deposit of weld metal from a single welding pass.

Welder

A person who performs manual or semiautomatic welding.

Welder Certification

Verification that a welder has demonstrated the ability to produce welds meeting prescribed standards.

Welding Position

The spatial relationship between the welder, the workpiece, and the welding equipment during welding.

Welding Procedure Specification (WPS)

A document that provides the required welding variables for a specific application to ensure repeatability by properly trained welders.

Weld Pool

The localized volume of molten metal in a weld prior to solidification.

Work Angle

The angle that the electrode makes with reference to the plane of the workpiece surface, measured perpendicular to the travel direction.

Work Lead

The electrical conductor connected to the workpiece, commonly referred to as the ground lead or work cable.

X

X-Ray Inspection

A nondestructive testing method using radiography to detect internal discontinuities in welds.

Y

Yield Strength

The stress at which a material exhibits a specified permanent deformation, typically 0.2% of the original length.

Y-Groove

A weld joint preparation where one member has a beveled edge and the other has a square edge, forming a Y-shaped groove.

Z

Z-Overlap

A workpiece positioning technique where adjacent parts are positioned in a staggered arrangement resembling the letter Z.

Zero-Clearance Torch

A welding torch designed for use in tight or confined spaces where a standard torch would not fit.
